# Music Theory — Project Memory

_Last updated: 2026-03-14_

## Overview
Mathematical exploration of music theory fundamentals — consonance, tuning systems, rhythm, timbre.
Location: ~/projects/music-theory/

## Completed Work
- **Tuning systems analysis** — comparative analysis of equal temperament, just intonation, Pythagorean, meantone
- **Waveform interference / polyrhythm analysis** — mathematical framework connecting pitch and rhythm
- **LCM analysis** — key insight: consonance = pattern that resolves quickly. LCM governs both pitch intervals and rhythmic polyrhythms identically. A perfect fifth (3:2) and a 3:2 polyrhythm are the same mathematical relationship expressed in different time scales.

## Next Steps
- **Timbre:** Odd vs even harmonics (why clarinet sounds different from oboe — missing even harmonics create "hollow" quality)
- **Melody as "memory interference"** — hypothesis: melodic tension/resolution is about how well the listener's pattern-matching system can predict the next note. Tension = prediction failure, resolution = prediction confirmed.

## Key Insight
Consonance is fundamentally about temporal pattern resolution. Low LCM = fast resolution = consonant. This applies identically to pitch (frequency ratios) and rhythm (beat ratios). Music theory's separate treatment of harmony and rhythm obscures their mathematical unity.
